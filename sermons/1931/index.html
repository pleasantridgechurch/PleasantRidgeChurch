1931 — “On Keeping the Door”

Home | Sermon Archive | Service Schedule | Attendance Ledger | Church History | Leadership

Text: Matthew 7:13–14, Psalm 24

Delivered: Winter 1931

Sermon

Beloved, there is a difference between an open door and a welcoming door.

Some think holiness is a thing that shines. I tell you holiness is a thing that keeps. It keeps the faithful. It keeps the stranger safe. It keeps the boundary where it belongs.

We speak often of hospitality, and rightly. But Scripture does not command us to be careless. The narrow gate is narrow for a reason, and the way that leads to life is not the way that every wandering voice will recognize.

So we keep the door in three ways:

  1. By name. We do not receive what refuses to be named.
  2. By witness. We do not receive what cannot bear being seen in the light.
  3. By time. We do not receive what demands to enter in haste.

If it is good, it can wait. If it is true, it can be spoken plainly. If it is from God, it will not fear the lamp.

And if a thing grows angry when you ask it to identify itself—then you have learned what it is.

Closing Prayer

Lord of the threshold, give us courage to be kind and wisdom to be careful. Teach our hands what to open and our hearts what to refuse. Amen.
