1942 — “On Remaining Watchful”

Home | Sermon Archive | Service Schedule | Attendance Ledger | Church History | Leadership

Text: Mark 13:33–37

Delivered: Late Winter 1942

Sermon

Beloved, the command to watch is not given only to those who expect danger. It is given to those who expect delay.

The household that sleeps without concern may be caught unaware, not because the master arrives suddenly, but because the servants have forgotten that waiting itself is a duty.

To remain watchful is not to live in fear. It is to live with attention.

Some signs are loud and demand response. Others are quiet and require patience. The danger is not missing the sign, but convincing ourselves that nothing is being asked of us.

We are told to keep watch at every hour, for the hour that seems least important is often the one in which responsibility is tested.

So we do not wait passively. We wait faithfully.

Closing Prayer

Lord of the watch and the waiting, keep us attentive in ordinary hours. Teach us to serve as though the moment matters, because it does. Amen.
