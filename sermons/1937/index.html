1937 — “On Returning to Ordinary Days”

Home | Sermon Archive | Service Schedule | Attendance Ledger | Church History | Leadership

Text: Ecclesiastes 3:1, Lamentations 3:22–23

Delivered: Spring 1937

Sermon

Beloved, there is a season for every matter under heaven, and there are times when the most faithful act is simply to return to ordinary days.

After periods of uncertainty, we are often tempted to search for meaning in what has passed. Yet Scripture reminds us that mercy is renewed each morning, not stored from yesterday.

The work of faith is not to preserve every experience, but to meet the present day with attention and gratitude.

We give thanks for calm seasons not because they explain what came before, but because they allow us to live rightly now.

So we return to our routines, our work, our worship, trusting that today’s faithfulness is sufficient.

Closing Prayer

God of new mercies, meet us again this morning. Teach us to receive what is given today, and to walk faithfully in ordinary time. Amen.
